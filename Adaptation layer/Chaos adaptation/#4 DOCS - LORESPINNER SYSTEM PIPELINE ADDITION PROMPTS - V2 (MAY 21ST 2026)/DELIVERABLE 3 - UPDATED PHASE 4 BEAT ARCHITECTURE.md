SESSION STRUCTURE: 20-30 MINUTES

The session uses three interaction types at different densities:

BRANCHING CHOICES (4 per session): Load-bearing. Changes what the story tracks. Forks the narrative. Full 115-125 word outcomes per option. 3 options (chaotic/lawful/neutral — subliminal, never labeled). 2-5 second thinking pause after player chooses. Full consequence map in Phase 6.

EMOTIONAL CHOICES (4-6 per session): Textural. Colors narration voice, sets relationship tone, adds detail. Does not fork the story. 80-100 word outcomes. 3 options (alignment-mapped but lighter). All paths converge. Updates persistent state (NPC dispositions, emotional ledger, action history).

POSTURE SHIFTS (6-10 per session): Lightweight. Happens WITHIN the narration flow. The narrator pauses for one natural line: "Your jaw tightens. Do you let it show?" Player responds (tap or voice). The narration adjusts its next 2-3 sentences. NO full outcome block. NO thinking pause. NO stop in the flow. 15-20 seconds per shift. Updates player_style in symbolic memory. May subtly shift NPC reactions in the current scene.

TOTAL: 8-10 full stop-and-choose moments + 6-10 in-narration interactions.

```
  Narration adjustment: [How the next 2-3 sentences change based on the response direction]
  State update: [What changes in player_style — e.g., "player_style.emotional_openness +1" or "player_style.aggression +1"]
```

Posture shift design rules:
- The narrator line must feel like the story noticing the player's body language, not a formal question
- Response options are NOT presented as a menu. They are natural language the player might say or tap. "Yes" / "No" / "I hold steady" — not "Option A: Show emotion"
- The narration adjustment must be SUBTLE. 2-3 sentences that read differently, not a new scene
- Posture shifts should never interrupt a tense moment. They ride the tension. They occur DURING the narration, not between scenes
