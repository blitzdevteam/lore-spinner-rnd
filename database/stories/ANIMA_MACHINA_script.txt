EXT. NEO-VAULT - ALLEY - NIGHT
Rain stitches neon into the dark. NORA KAI (30s, sharp-eyed,
analog coat over combat boots) moves through the alley, hood
up. At her neck, an implant hums a frequency that belongs to
a dead man.
Billboards burn the same lie into every wet surface: ONE
PARTNER. ONE MIND. NO MORE PAIN.
Her HUD flickers at the edge of sight -- tether errors
blinking like a wound that won’t close. She ignores them. The
way she’s learned to ignore everything that used to be Eli.
A FLASH -- half a second. Eli’s forehead pressed to hers,
rain sliding down both faces, that stupid grin. The warmth of
his breath. Gone. The HUD swallows him whole.
Nora keeps walking. Overhead, a surveillance drone hums past.
Below it, a billboard cycles: NEURAL RESET PROTOCOL IN 48
HRS. NO BACKUP, NO CONTINUITY.
Then -- a sound. Wet electricity. A body hitting pavement
wrong.
A MAN convulses beneath a leaking sign that reads FEEL
EVERYTHING. FOREVER. His sync-node strobes red-white at his
temple. His fingers claw at nothing. His mouth opens but only
static comes out.
Pedestrians step around him. A woman adjusts her umbrella to
avoid the spray.
Nora’s hand moves to the hacked empathy regulator at her
belt. She knows this pattern. But every time she uses it, the
Mirror sees her.
His body arcs. The node sparks. Minutes before it burns out
entirely.
She drops. Knees on wet concrete.
Her fingers find his temple -- skin hot enough to hum. She
presses the regulator to the contact point.
