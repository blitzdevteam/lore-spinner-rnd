DRIFTHEART
Written by
Thomas Wittmer
Based on A LoreSpinner Original

FADE IN:
EXT. DEEP SPACE - THE SHATTERED WORLD OF ERYTHANE
Black. Then light, wrong light. A planet that no longer
exists as one thing.
ERYTHANE. A world split into seven massive fragments, each
the size of a continent, tumbling in slow rotation around a
molten core that still burns between them. Rivers of magma
arc through the vacuum, luminous veins connecting severed
limbs.
Chunks of planetary crust, some the size of cities, cartwheel
through the void. Dust clouds thick enough to swallow light.
A DROPSHIP appears. Small. Fragile. Threading between
continent-sized fragments.
INT. DROPSHIP - CARGO HOLD - CONTINUOUS
Six people in the hold. Salvagers. Freelancers. The kind of
people who take jobs that come with a body-count disclaimer.
They sit on fold-down benches bolted to the hull. Weapons
stowed. Gear strapped. Faces lit by amber emergency strips
and the pale glow of helmet HUDs cycling through pre-drop
diagnostics.
KATARIA DARANA (21), sharp-eyed, lean, dark hair pulled back
tight. She checks her breathing rig for the third time. Her
fingers are steady. Her jaw is not.
Across from her: DRAETH VORN (29), coiled precision in a
battered flight jacket. He polishes a sidearm like it's
ritual. His eyes track Kataria. Not watching over her.
Watching her.
HARSK (40s, heavyset) runs a final check on his shotgun. A
child's DRAWING is clipped to his chest harness, stick
figures beneath a crayon sun. He catches Kataria looking.
Tucks it under the strap without a word.
SENNA (30s, wiry, shaved head) runs her thumb along a catalog
of creature GLYPHS tattooed on her forearm. Field names. Kill
records. She's the one they bring when nobody knows what
they're walking into.
The remaining two are DOSS and KELLAN, younger, greener.
Already pale.
The ship SHUDDERS. Hard. Everyone grabs hold.

A DEEP GROAN runs through the hull. Metal flexing. The lights
flicker.
KATARIA
How long to the fragment?
DRAETH
(checking a wrist
display)
Four minutes. Maybe less.
He looks at her. Holds the look one beat too long.
DRAETH(CONT’D)
Stay close to me in there.
KATARIA
I can handle myself.
DRAETH
I know you can. That's not why.
He doesn't finish. She lets it go. Out the narrow viewport
beside her, the shattered world fills the frame. Continent
fragments the color of rust and char, backlit by the exposed
core. Beautiful. Terrible.
KATARIA
What are we actually looking for
down there?
DRAETH
What the client paid us to find.
KATARIA
That's not an answer.
DRAETH
It's the one that keeps us all
employed.
She holds his gaze. He doesn't blink. Something unspoken sits
between them, and she lets it pass. For now.
PILOT (O.S.)
(over comms, strained)
Thirty seconds to atmosphere. Suit
integrity is mandatory. It's going
to get rough.
Everyone locks helmets. Visors seal with pressurized HISSES.
Draeth reaches across the aisle. Taps Kataria's knee once.
She nods.

Then the ship hits the atmosphere.
EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - UPPER ATMOSPHERE - CONTINUOUS
The dropship punches through a wall of superheated gas.
Flames engulf the hull. The ship SCREAMS, metal against
friction, turbulence slamming it sideways.
Chunks of orbital debris burn alongside them. Some larger
than the ship. A piece of what was once a building tumbles
past, trailing fire.
INT. DROPSHIP - CARGO HOLD - CONTINUOUS
VIOLENT SHAKING. Cargo containers strain against their
straps. The freelancers grip their harnesses with white
knuckles.
DOSS
This is insane, this is.
KELLAN
Shut up and hold on.
A MASSIVE IMPACT.
A SHRIEK of tearing metal that goes through bone. The entire
ship lurches sideways. Sparks cascade from ruptured conduits.
Then the hull BREACHES.
A section of the port wall RIPS OPEN, a jagged mouth of
twisted metal two meters wide. Atmosphere HOWLS outward.
Everything not bolted down rockets toward the gap.
DOSS. His harness buckle, damaged by the impact, SNAPS. He
screams. Slides. Claws at the deck plating. His fingers find
nothing.
He's PULLED THROUGH THE BREACH. Gone. Swallowed by fire and
void.
Kataria lunges for him. Too late. Her hand closes on empty
air.
A cargo container breaks free of its straps, two tons of
metal swinging. It catches KELLAN across the chest. CRUSHES
him against the bulkhead. He doesn't scream. There isn't
time.
SENNA
Bulkheads! Seal the bulkheads!

Harsk slams the emergency override. Blast doors DROP, sealing
the breach section. The howling stops. Pressure stabilizes.
Smoke. Sparking wires. Two dead in under five seconds.
The ship is still falling.
DRAETH
Cockpit. Someone get to the
cockpit!
Kataria is already moving. Unclipping her harness. Pulling
herself forward through the lurching corridor, hand over
hand, gravity shifting as the ship tumbles.
INT. DROPSHIP - CORRIDOR - CONTINUOUS
Kataria fights down the narrow passage. Ceiling panels fall.
A conduit bursts, scalding vapor. She ducks, keeps moving.
The cockpit door is buckled. She KICKS it. Again. AGAIN.
It gives.
INT. DROPSHIP - COCKPIT - CONTINUOUS
Both pilots are dead.
Pilot 1 is slumped over the controls. A spear of debris
punched through the viewport and through him. Decompression
foam has auto-sealed the breach, but it sealed around a
corpse.
Pilot 2 is in his chair. Neck at a wrong angle. Console
crushed inward against his chest.
Kataria freezes. One second. The longest second of her life.
Alarms. Everywhere. The altimeter is spinning. The ground is
coming.
She shoves Pilot 2's body aside. Drops into the blood-slicked
seat. Grabs the yoke.
KATARIA
(to herself)
Okay. Okay okay okay.
Through the cracked viewport, the surface of Fragment Seven
rushing up. Jagged mountain ranges. Volcanic plains. No
runway. No landing zone.
Two options. She can see them both.

OPTION ONE: A narrow canyon that cuts between two mountain
ridges. Sheltered, but tight. One wrong correction and they
clip a wall and disintegrate.
OPTION TWO: An open volcanic flat. Wide, but the surface
ripples with heat distortion. Lava flows beneath a thin
crust. They'd land and sink.
She pulls the yoke LEFT. Hard. Into the canyon.
In her side pouch, the containment cylinder HUMS. Faint. The
first time it's made a sound since boarding.
KATARIA(CONT’D)
(over comms)
Hold on to something. Now.
The ship banks. Metal GROANS.
EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - CANYON - CONTINUOUS
The dropship screams through the canyon. Winglets SHEAR OFF
against rock walls in showers of sparks. The belly scrapes a
ridge, a rooster tail of stone and fire.
INT. DROPSHIP - COCKPIT - CONTINUOUS
Kataria fights the yoke. Every muscle. The ship wants to spin
and she will not let it.
She sees it. The canyon opens ahead into a narrow shelf of
flat rock. Not much. Enough.
She cuts the engines. Fires retro-thrusters. The ship SLAMS.
Impact. BLACK.
EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - CRASH SITE - MOMENTS LATER
Silence. Dust settling. The dropship is a ruin, belly-down on
the rock shelf, hull cracked open. One engine still burns.
Smoke pours from every seam.
A long beat. Nothing moves.
Then the emergency hatch BLOWS outward.

EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - CRASH SITE - CONTINUOUS
KATARIA climbs out first. Helmet sealed. Breathing hard. She
turns back and helps DRAETH through. Then HARSK. Then SENNA.
Four. Out of eight.
They stand in the wreckage. The air beyond their helmets is a
haze of sulfur-yellow particulate.
The ground beneath their boots is cracked basalt, veined with
dull orange, heat from below. Every few seconds, a deep THRUM
vibrates through the rock.
Kataria looks up.
THE SKY.
The other fragments of Erythane hang overhead. Close enough
to see mountain ranges on their surfaces.
They rotate slowly, casting continent-sized shadows that
sweep across the terrain. Between them, the exposed planetary
core pulses, a bruised sun of molten iron and dying light.
She stares too long. Draeth's hand on her shoulder breaks it.
DRAETH
The cathedral. One point two klicks
northwest. Built into the base of
that ridge.
He points. Through the haze, barely visible, a MOUNTAIN. And
at its base, carved into the living rock: the faint outline
of massive architectural forms. Spires. Arches. The
CATHEDRAL.
Kataria notices Draeth's wrist display. The nav-data on his
screen doesn't match the job briefing they all downloaded.
Different coordinates. A deeper waypoint. He angles it away
before she can read the rest.
She clocks it. Doesn't say anything.
KATARIA
Then we move.
DRAETH
Stay tight. Stay armed. This
planet's been dead for a thousand
years but the readings on the
descent were.

He trails off. Looks at the cracked ground beneath them.
Something flickers in the fissures. A dull, ember-orange
glow. Moving.
DRAETH(CONT’D)
Wrong.
KATARIA
Wrong how?
DRAETH
Thermal signatures. Organic. Moving
in patterns.
SENNA
(checking her forearm
glyphs, then the
fissures)
Nothing in the catalogs moves like
that.
Beat.
KATARIA
Let's move. Fast.
EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - VOLCANIC PLAINS - CONTINUOUS
The four freelancers move across the blasted terrain in a
loose formation. Draeth on point. Kataria beside him. Harsk
and Senna covering the rear.
The landscape is hell.
Fissures split the ground every few meters, some narrow
enough to step over, some wide enough to swallow a person.
Volcanic vents hiss jets of superheated gas. The rock itself
is unstable, plates of basalt shifting and grinding.
Kataria falls into step with Draeth. Keeps her voice low.
KATARIA
You've been here before.
DRAETH
What makes you say that?
KATARIA
You haven't looked at the map once
since we started walking.

He doesn't answer. Just adjusts his grip on his weapon and
picks up the pace. She watches his back.
A hundred meters out. Two hundred. Three.
Harsk stops.
HARSK
Hold.
Everyone freezes.
HARSK(CONT’D)
You feel that?
The ground is vibrating. Not the deep planetary thrum from
before. Something else. Localized. Getting stronger.
SENNA
Under us. Something's under us.
A CRACK splits the rock three meters to their left. Not a
fissure. Something FORCING its way through from below. The
basalt buckles upward, then SHATTERS.
A mass of fluid black pulls itself from the earth. Veins of
burning orange and red crack across its surface. It radiates
heat, visible heat distortion warping the air around it.
The shape is almost humanoid. Almost. It shifts and reforms
constantly, limbs extending and retracting, a body that can't
decide what it is. It has no face. But it orients toward
them. It knows they're there.
Then another crack. Behind them. Another to the right. FOUR
MORE pulling themselves free. Surrounding them.
Senna's eyes go wide. Her thumb traces the blank space at the
end of her forearm catalog. Room for one more.
SENNA(CONT’D)
(backing up, weapon
raised)
Cinders.
She fires. Her pulse rifle hits the nearest creature center-
mass. The round PUNCHES through. Black fluid sprays,
instantly igniting into orange flame where it hits air. The
creature staggers. Reforms. Keeps coming.
DRAETH
The bright spots. Hit the ember
lines!

Draeth fires. Precise. Two shots into the glowing fissures
that crack across the creature's torso.
It SHRIEKS, a sound of superheated metal quenched in water,
and collapses into a pool of hissing black.
But there are more. Always more. Cracks opening everywhere
now. The ground itself is birthing them.
KATARIA
Run! Cathedral! Now!
They run. Firing behind them. The Cinders pursue. Not fast,
but relentless. They flow over terrain, THROUGH fissures,
reforming on the other side. One reaches for Senna.
Its hand closes on her arm. Where it touches, her suit MELTS.
She screams. Draeth spins, fires point-blank into the
creature's head. It drops. Senna stumbles forward, arm
scorched but moving.
DRAETH
Keep going.
They run harder. The cathedral growing larger ahead. Close
now.
A fissure ERUPTS directly in their path. Three Cinders rise
from it simultaneously. Harsk doesn't slow down. He barrels
into them, fires his shotgun into the first, drives his
armored shoulder into the second.
HARSK
Get to the door! I'll hold them!
KATARIA
Harsk.
HARSK
I got kids who need me to buy you
thirty seconds, Darana. Don't waste
them.
He fires again. And again. A Cinder latches onto his back.
Another onto his leg. He ROARS. Keeps firing. Buying them
seconds.
Kataria, Draeth, and Senna sprint the last hundred meters.
Behind them, Harsk goes down. The Cinders swarm him. His
weapon discharges twice more, then stops. The ember-glow
intensifies where he fell.
He doesn't get back up.

EXT. FRAGMENT SEVEN - CATHEDRAL ENTRANCE - CONTINUOUS
The cathedral doors. Massive. Thirty meters tall. Carved from
the mountain itself, black stone etched with patterns that
seem to move in peripheral vision. VEIL GLYPHS.
Ancient. Geometric. Spiraling inward toward a central locking
mechanism.
No handle. No obvious entry point. Just the glyphs and the
mountain.
Behind them, the Cinders. Dozens now. Flowing across the
terrain. Two hundred meters and closing.
SENNA
(scanning the creatures,
then the door)
They're massing at the fissure
line. Sixty seconds before the
first wave hits us.
KATARIA
Working on it.
She studies the locking mechanism. A series of concentric
rings carved into the stone, each bearing different glyph
sequences. They're meant to be aligned.
DRAETH
The rings rotate. You need to match
the harmonic sequence.
KATARIA
How do you know that?
DRAETH
Job briefing.
KATARIA
Our job briefing said "recover
salvage from a ruin." Not one word
about Veil ciphers.
He doesn't answer. The Cinders are screaming behind them. No
time to press it.
KATARIA(CONT’D)
Which harmonic sequence? There are
six rings and at least forty
glyphs.
She stops. Looks closer. The innermost ring has a pattern she
recognizes. Not from a database. Not from a briefing.

From somewhere older. The margins of old star charts she
studied as a girl. Shapes she traced with her finger on a
balcony overlooking the void, half a lifetime ago.
Her hand moves before her brain catches up. She rotates the
inner ring. CLICK. The glyphs pulse with faint blue light.
SENNA
(weapon up, eyes on the
plain)
Forty seconds.
Second ring. She reads the pattern, finds the complement.
Rotates. CLICK.
Third ring. Fourth. Faster now. Her hands moving with a
certainty that frightens her.
Fifth ring. CLICK.
Sixth ring. She hesitates.
Two possible alignments. The first is mathematical. Clean.
The glyphs line up by symmetry. It's the answer anyone with
enough time could solve.
The second makes no logical sense. The glyphs don't match.
But something in her BODY knows it. The same way she knew the
canyon was right.
SENNA(CONT’D)
Kataria. Twenty seconds.
She can hear them. The metal-quench shriek. Dozens of voices.
She chooses the one her hands choose. The one that costs her
the certainty of logic.
CLICK.
Deep inside the mountain, past corridors they haven't reached
yet, the shard on its pedestal FLARES. A pulse of blue-white
that travels through stone. Through the floor. Through
Kataria's boots.
She feels it. Doesn't understand it. Not yet.
The doors GROAN. Stone grinding against stone. Ancient
mechanisms firing for the first time in millennia. The thirty-
meter doors begin to swing inward.
DRAETH
Go. Go. Go.

They squeeze through the widening gap. Senna last, turning to
fire a final burst at the nearest Cinder. Twenty meters away.
Reaching.
The doors SLAM SHUT. The sound echoes through stone.
Silence.
INT. CATHEDRAL OF THE VEIL - ENTRANCE HALL - CONTINUOUS
Darkness. Then light. Soft. Blue-white. Coming from the walls
themselves.
VEIL GLYPHS. Thousands of them. Carved into every surface.
Floor, walls, the vaulted ceiling fifty meters above. They
pulse. Slow rhythms.
Stone arches buttressed by something that isn’t stone.
Pillars carved with spiraling glyph-work that extends beyond
sight. The floor is polished obsidian, mirror-smooth. Part
cathedral. Part alien machine.
Senna catches her breath. Draeth checks his weapon. Kataria
does neither.
She stares at the glyphs. She's shaking. Not from fear.
KATARIA
I've seen these before.
DRAETH
(too quickly)
The Veil Glyphs are documented in
salvage databases. You've probably.
KATARIA
No. Not in a database. I've, seen
them.
He doesn't respond. His hand, the one still gripping his
weapon, relaxes. A man who just got the answer to a question
he's been carrying for a long time.
KATARIA(CONT’D)
You knew I'd recognize them.
DRAETH
The artifact is in the central
chamber. Let's move.
KATARIA
That's the third time you've dodged
a question, Draeth.

DRAETH
And that sound you hear is the
clock running out on all of us.
Move.
She holds his gaze. Two seconds. Three. Then she moves. But
the way she positions herself as they walk, she keeps him in
her peripheral vision. Not behind her. Never behind her
again.
INT. CATHEDRAL OF THE VEIL - CENTRAL CHAMBER - CONTINUOUS
They enter the heart of the cathedral.
The chamber is vast. Circular. The ceiling opens into a
natural shaft in the mountain, and through it, the dying core
of Erythane burns far above.
Its light falls in a single column onto the chamber floor.
And there, at the center, floating above a pedestal of raw
obsidian: THE DRIFTHEART SHARD.
The size of a fist. Crystalline. Its interior swirls with
light that has no source. It rotates slowly. It hums, a low,
resonant frequency that vibrates in the chest, not the ears.
Ancient. Alive.
The Veil Glyphs across every surface of the chamber pulse in
synchronization with the shard's rotation. The whole room
breathes with it.
SENNA
That's what we came for?
DRAETH
Don't touch anything. Not yet.
Kataria takes a step forward. The glyphs on the floor beneath
her boot FLARE, brighter than the others. A ripple of light
cascades outward from where she stands, racing across the
floor, up the walls, across the ceiling.
Everyone freezes.
She takes another step. Another flare. The glyphs are
responding to HER. Draeth and Senna stand on the same floor
and nothing happens. But where Kataria walks, the cathedral
wakes up.
The hum from the shard INTENSIFIES. Changes pitch. A note of
recognition.

Draeth exhales. Slow. Controlled. He lowers his weapon.
Closes his eyes for one beat. A man whose long bet just paid
off.
DRAETH(CONT’D)
(soft)
Go to it, Kata. It's waiting for
you.
KATARIA
You don't seem surprised.
DRAETH
Please. Just trust me. One more
time.
The weight of those words. She hears it. She files it.
Kataria crosses the chamber. Each step ignites more glyphs.
By the time she reaches the pedestal, the entire room is
blazing, every symbol lit, every surface alive with cascading
light.
She reaches out.
Her fingers close around the DRIFTHEART SHARD.
And the world BREAKS.
A SHOCKWAVE detonates from the pedestal. A ring of visible
force SLAMS outward in every direction. Kataria is thrown
backward, the shard clutched to her chest.
The glyphs go from blue-white to BLINDING. The hum becomes a
ROAR. The cathedral SHAKES. The building itself is reacting.
Defending. ATTACKING.
Columns of energy ERUPT from the glyph-work. Random.
Devastating.
Senna spins toward Kataria, moving to cover her. One boot
still planted on the obsidian. She reaches out.
A column of Veilfire tears through the floor between them.
It rips through Senna. UNMADE. Disintegrated in a flash of
blue-white.
KATARIA
No!
The chamber is collapsing. The ceiling shaft cracking.
Massive blocks of ancient stone falling. Energy arcing
between the walls.

Draeth. Running toward Kataria. A column CRACKS. A section of

enough.
The debris catches his left leg. CRUSHES it. He goes down
hard. Face-first against the obsidian floor.
KATARIA(CONT’D)
Draeth!
She scrambles to him. Pulls at the debris. It doesn't move.
His leg is pinned, shattered under tons of ancient stone.
He grabs her arm. Pulls her close. His face through the

DRAETH
(through gritted teeth)
Kata. Listen to me.
KATARIA
I'm getting you out.
DRAETH
Listen.

DRAETH(CONT’D)

Ashen trace, through the Veil. I

DRAETH
Yes.

DRAETH

(quiet, worse than

Harsk had kids.

DRAETH

Energy cascading.

Almost.

A CRACK. The floor between them splits. Energy surges upward

DRAETH(CONT’D)
