FADE IN:
[INTERACTIVE COLD OPEN — SECOND PERSON]
The rain hasn't stopped for hours. You feel it running down
the back of your neck, soaking through silk into skin. Your
shoes are somewhere behind you — you stopped noticing when.
You are Akira Kawasaki. And this is the last building you
will ever enter as yourself.
Nocturne Ltd. The facade is corporate. Forgettable. The kind
of building designed to be passed without seeing. But you see
it. You've been staring at it for eleven minutes, rain
pooling around your bare feet, reading the words you already
memorized from the packet: PARTICIPATION IS VOLUNTARY.
NOCTURNE LTD. IS NOT LIABLE UNDER NATIONAL OR INTERNATIONAL
JURISDICTION.
Your phone is already wiped. Your apartment lease is void.
The email you sent three hours ago has already detonated
somewhere inside the Kawasaki Group, and there is no version
of tomorrow where your name still means what it meant
yesterday.
The buzzer glows. A small red eye, waiting.
Behind you: the city, and everything it now knows about you.
Ahead: a door that only opens once.
[BRANCHING CHOICE #1 — THE THRESHOLD]
The buzzer glows red. A small eye in the concrete, patient
and unblinking. Behind you, Tokyo hums — wet neon, distant
trains, the last sounds of a city that already knows your
name for the wrong reasons.
You have been standing here for eleven minutes.
The rain is warm. Your feet are bare against the pavement.
The packet said to arrive with nothing sentimental. You kept
the jade cicada anyway — your sister's, pressed against your
collarbone beneath your shirt.
The door will open once. It will not open again from the
inside.

What do you do?
A)
! Rei opens the door to a woman who doesn't hesitate.
Nocturne logs: "composed, voluntary, no observed distress."
Intake moves fast. She is categorized before she can be
curious.
B)
! Rei opens the door to a 97-second delay flag on his tablet.
He studies her face before speaking. Intake slows. They watch
her more carefully.
C)
! Rei hears her name through the intercom before he opens the
door. Recalibration in his posture. They expected compliance.
They got a declaration.
[ALL PATHS CONVERGE: The lobby. Rei. The welcome screen.]
INT. NOCTURNE LTD. — LOBBY — NIGHT
Fluorescent. Cold. No reception desk. The air tastes recycled
— institutional, bloodless.
A wall-mounted display loops a digital welcome screen. It
glitches faintly.
INSERT — THE SCREEN:
WELCOME TO NOCTURNE LTD.
PARTICIPATION IS VOLUNTARY.
NOCTURNE LTD. IS NOT LIABLE UNDER NATIONAL OR INTERNATIONAL
JURISDICTION.
Below it, faintly etched in the glass beneath the screen, a
worn government seal — partially scraped off. As if someone
tried to erase history but didn't finish.
REI MIKAMI (30s) waits in a pressed suit. Pen in hand.
Notebook open. The smile he gives her is thin. Professional.
REI
You're on time.
Akira nods. Doesn't sit.

AKIRA
What happens now?
REI
We begin.
He clicks his pen. The sound echoes.
A flicker on the wall-mounted display. For a split second a
black glyph, ancient and insect-like, flashes across the
screen: !".
Akira tenses. Eyes narrow — not recognition, but the instinct
to.
REI(CONT’D)
(beat)
Ignore that. Just legacy code. Old
systems still bleeding through.
The screen clears. Clean corporate blue. Akira says nothing.
But she's seen it.
INT. FACILITY — HALLWAY — MOMENTS LATER
Long corridor. White tile, stainless steel. The buzz of
electricity hums beneath the silence. Their footsteps do not
echo. This place was built not to be remembered.
REI
Phones. Wallet. Identification.
Akira hands him everything without protest. He drops each
item into a plastic envelope.
He reaches for the necklace. The jade cicada — small, cool,
worn smooth by years against skin.
REI(CONT’D)
Sentimental?
[EXPRESSIVE CHOICE — THE JADE CICADA]
The jade cicada — small, cool, worn smooth by years against
skin that isn't yours anymore. Your sister wore it the last
summer you were both still whole.
"Sentimental?" he asks.

How do you let go?
A)
! The cicada disappears into plastic. Rei seals it without
looking at you. He writes: "compliant, low affect." He's
wrong, and you let him be.
B)
! Rei watches your fingers close and open. He doesn't rush
you. When you place it in the envelope, his pen pauses over
the intake form. He writes nothing.
C)
! "Not anymore," he says. But his hand hesitates over the
seal. For half a breath, something human crosses his face —
then it's gone. He files it. But he heard you.
[ALL PATHS CONVERGE: The Induction Room.]
INT. INDUCTION ROOM — NIGHT
A room meant to look medical but not sterile. That cold-warm
confusion found in hotels and funeral homes.
YUMI TAKEDA (late 20s) stands behind a terminal. Unsmiling.
Her hair is too neat. Her nails too clean.
YUMI
Strip down to underclothes.
Akira doesn't move.
YUMI(CONT’D)
I assume you read the packet.
AKIRA
I read it.
YUMI
Then you know what we need.
Akira takes her time. Blazer. Blouse. Skirt. Each item folded
precisely and laid on the chair.
REI
We ask for no lies here. Only the
self you intend to kill.
Akira meets his gaze. Cold. Furious. And afraid.

AKIRA
You want a confession?
YUMI
No. We want your permission.
Beat. Akira nods once. Yumi steps forward with a scanner.
Moves it over Akira's body.
The screen behind her flickers with pulsing lines. Brainwave
graphs. Thermal overlays. Lines of kanji auto-populate in
red.
YUMI(CONT’D)
No pacemaker. No pregnancy. No
record of psychiatric detainment.
REI
Physical trauma. Lower left
thoracic scarring.
AKIRA
Knife.
YUMI
Yours?
AKIRA
(a breath)
No.
Yumi doesn't write it down. She already knows it doesn't
matter.
INT. INDUCTION ROOM — CONTINUOUS
REI
Akira Kawasaki. Final request
confirmed.
He slides a folder toward her. Thick. Paper-clipped.
REI(CONT’D)
Sign and thumbprint. You may choose
to speak a final sentence into
record.
Akira flips through it. Her eyes pause at one section:
MOTIVE.
Her pen hovers.

[EXPRESSIVE CHOICE — THE MOTIVE]
The document is thick. Paper-clipped. Legal language designed
to make consent feel like gravity — inevitable, impersonal,
already happened.
Your pen moves through the pages. Signature. Thumbprint.
Waiver of identity. Waiver of return.
Then: MOTIVE. A single blank line.
What do you write?
A)
! Yumi's eyes flicker. Not pity — recognition. She's heard
versions of this before, but something in the way you wrote
it makes her look at you one beat longer than protocol
allows.
B)
! The pen lifts. The ink dries. Rei reads it and his
expression doesn't change — but his hand stalls on the page,
just slightly. You've given them something true.
C)
! Yumi tilts her head. Rei glances at the empty line, then at
you. No one has left it blank before. The system doesn't know
how to categorize silence.
[ALL PATHS CONVERGE: Transfer begins.]
INT. TRANSFER CORRIDOR — NIGHT
Dim, narrow. Metallic hum. The hallway feels endless — a
tunnel of waiting.
Akira walks, flanked by two silent attendants. No words
exchanged.
Up ahead, another subject. Male. Mid-30s. SWEATING. SHAKING.
He looks over his shoulder, sees Akira, and SNAPS.
SUBJECT
No — NO! This isn't what they said!
He SPINS, lands a brutal elbow into one attendant's throat.
The man crumples.

Chaos erupts. Screams echo down the corridor. A security
strobe hits — harsh, white, pulsing.
The other attendant tackles the subject. Fists flying. A
syringe hits the floor. Shatters.
Akira stops. Unmoving. Watching.
The subject locks eyes with her. Bloody. Wild.
SUBJECT(CONT’D)
Don't let them —
He's yanked backward and out of frame. His scream cuts off.
Silence returns. Except for the hum.
Akira blinks. And keeps walking.
A DOOR opens ahead. Swallowing her.
INT. COMMON ROOM — NIGHT
[BREATH BEAT — SCHELL RULE: 8-10 MINUTE MARK]
A cafeteria space. Pale wood. Soft music filtered through
speakers recessed in the ceiling. Everything is a little too
clean. People sit in groups of twos and threes. Smiling. Too
still.
Akira enters. Her shoes make no sound against the vinyl
floor. The room registers her presence, but no one looks.
Yumi steps forward from the corner, like she was waiting for
this moment.
YUMI
Relax. They're friendly. In theory.
Yumi leads her to a small circular table. A WOMAN (20s,
polite, pretty, blank) sits with her hands folded neatly in
her lap.
WOMAN
Relocation due to stalker
harassment. Duration, indefinite.
She smiles. Perfect. Hollow.
WOMAN(CONT’D)
I'm grateful to be here.
Akira nods. Her breath catches.

Yumi moves behind the woman — just for a beat. The woman's
lips shift. A crack in the glaze.
WOMAN(CONT’D)
(whispers)
Get out. They're MUKUROKAI.
Her smile resumes, as if it never left. Her eyes never moved.
A SOUND — somewhere in the walls. A girl's laugh. Breathless,
joyful, wrong for this space. The laugh of a child chasing
another child through water on a summer afternoon.
Akira freezes. She knows that laugh. She grew up chasing it.
She turns. Nothing. Just the hum of the building, breathing.
[EXPRESSIVE CHOICE — THE MUKUROKAI WARNING]
The laugh fades. The woman sits perfectly still. You are
being watched.
What do you do?
A)
! The woman's smile tightens. Her eyes widen — just enough to
show white. She doesn't answer. But under the table, her
fingers brush yours. Brief. Electric. A warning delivered
through skin. Then she stands and walks away. You never see
her again.
B)
! You catalogue her the way you were taught to study a
painting — not for beauty, but for what the artist tried to
conceal. She leaves. But the impression stays — and when you
see a face like hers again, you'll know where to look.
C)
! Something shifts in her expression. Not fear. Surprise. She
wasn't expecting to be understood. Your smile holds. Hers
falters. For one second, two real people sit across from each
other in a room full of ghosts.
[ALL PATHS CONVERGE: The Whisper Room.]
INT. THE WHISPER ROOM — NIGHT
A small booth. Eggshell walls. No mirrors. No visible
surveillance. But there's the sense of being watched from
inside your own skin.

Akira steps in. The door seals behind her with a pneumatic
hiss. The ceiling light dims.
A speaker embedded somewhere in the walls crackles softly.
First: SHIN'S VOICE.
SHIN (V.O.)
You should learn to keep secrets if
you want to stay important...
She winces.
Then: her sister.
SISTER (V.O.)
Did you forget me?
A low-frequency hum begins. Deep. Vibrational. Her
fingernails twitch involuntarily. She scratches at her scar.
Harder now. Breath short.
Then, a third voice. Genderless. Timeless. Something inhuman.
VOICE (V.O.)
MUKUROKAI is watching.
It sounds like it's coming from inside her own lungs.
Her body tightens. She presses her hands over her ears, but
the sound is not in the room. It's in her.
The tone escalates. Not louder — deeper. Molecular.
Then — silence.
The door slides open. Yumi stands there, immaculate. The
hallway behind her is a tomb.
YUMI
Longer than most.
Akira says nothing. She steps out.
INT. TRANSITION CHAMBER — NIGHT
A narrow room of obsidian black. No walls — only reflections.
The floor pulses faintly beneath Akira's feet, like it's
breathing.
A HUM builds from the walls. Not mechanical. Organic. Like a
throat clearing.

Yumi stands across from her. No file this time. Just her
presence.
YUMI
Final clearance requires silence.
AKIRA
I thought I passed.
YUMI
That was compliance. This is
surrender.
Akira meets her gaze. Something in her wants to fight.
Something else wants to disappear.
YUMI(CONT’D)
Step forward.
Akira does. The HUM deepens. Her reflection flickers.
YUMI(CONT’D)
Name?
AKIRA
Akira Kawasaki.
YUMI
No. Name?
[BRANCHING CHOICE #2 — THE IDENTITY SURRENDER]
[MORAL-WEIGHT CHOICE — NO CORRECT ANSWER]
You feel the weight of it — every version of yourself you've
ever been, pressed into a single syllable. The hum is inside
your ribs now. Yumi is close enough that you can feel the
warmth of her breath. She's waiting. The system is waiting.
The room is waiting.

What do you say?
A)
   Reflects: PRAGMATISM
! Yumi presses two fingers to Akira's forehead — anointing or
branding. "Welcome home," she whispers. But her eyes are
disappointed. She wanted you to fight. The system accepts
compliance. But in the obsidian reflection, your image
lingers one frame longer — as if the reflection refused even
though you didn't.
B)
   Reflects: INTEGRITY
! The hum fractures. Yumi's composure cracks — not anger,
something rawer. She steps back. "You will regret this," she
says, but her voice shakes, and regret is not what lives in
her eyes. It's hunger. System flags: NONCOMPLIANT. ELEVATED
RISK. OBSERVE.
C)
   Reflects: CUNNING
! Yumi's breath catches. The protocol has no response. For
one unguarded second the mask drops — not the professional
one, the personal one. She was once someone else too. The
power dynamic between you is no longer vertical.
[ALL PATHS CONVERGE: First night in the facility.]
INT. AKIRA'S ASSIGNED APARTMENT — NIGHT
Spartan. Too clean. Generic feminine touches applied like
paint. A silk scarf draped. A pink toothbrush. A photo of
friends she doesn't know.
Akira sits on the bed, still clothed. The ID card in her
hand. The name on it etched in flawless Helvetica.
She runs her thumb across it. Again. And again.
She breathes.
She moves to the bathroom. Flicks on the light.
Looks in the mirror —
Her reflection STARES BACK. But not synchronously. It's
delayed. A breath behind. Or ahead.

Then — the reflection SMILES.
But she hasn't.
She blinks.
The smile is gone.
The mirror CRACKS.
SMASH CUT TO:
INT. TRANSFER CHAMBER — NIGHT
[RESOLUTION BEAT]
The Transfer Chamber is circular. Velvet-black. Sound dies at
the walls. The light beneath your feet pulses in a ring — a
halo inverted, glowing upward into nothing.
You've been standing here for what feels like hours. It may
have been minutes. Time doesn't behave in this room.
The door hisses.
Yumi enters carrying a metal briefcase. She opens it without
ceremony. Inside: a slim surgical device. A syringe. The
fluid is black and viscous — not still. It shifts inside the
glass, sluggish, metallic, as if responding to warmth.
YUMI
Final conversion.
Her voice is clinical. Her hands are steady. But she won't
look at the syringe when she lifts it.
YUMI(CONT’D)
You disappear so you may be remade.
You stare at the needle.
AKIRA
I didn't understand the terms.
YUMI
You understood enough.
She steps closer. The syringe catches light. Her other hand
reaches for your arm — her fingers warm against your skin.
Not the grip of a handler. The hold of someone who knows what
this costs.

AKIRA
What do I become?
YUMI
Someone we can trust.
The room hums. The light pulses.
You look at her hand on your arm. At the syringe. At the
woman holding it, who has done this before — to others, to
herself, to someone whose face you keep seeing in mirrors.
And something in you — not a thought, not a decision,
something you couldn't stop if you wanted to — says no.
You move.
The briefcase hits the floor. The syringe shatters. Black
fluid hisses against the tile, burning faint smoke.
No alarms sound.
No footsteps in the corridor.
The system does not react.
You stand in the center of the chamber, barefoot on warm
tile, shards of glass around your feet, black smoke curling
upward, and the silence is so complete you can hear your own
pulse — steady, alive, yours.
Yumi hasn't moved. Her hand still hangs where Akira's arm
was. Her face is stripped open — not anger, not protocol, not
the careful mask she's worn all night. What's behind it is
fear. Not of Akira.
Of what she just proved is possible.
INT. TRANSFER CHAMBER — CONTINUOUS
[SESSION-END HOOK]
You breathe. Once. The glass crunches under your feet. Yumi's
eyes follow you but she doesn't move, doesn't speak, doesn't
reach for the alarm she should have pulled thirty seconds
ago.
You are still here. Still you.
Above her — the mirrored dome flickers.
A glyph. Ancient. Insect-like. #$. It pulses once across the
curved surface — not projected from a machine.

From beneath Akira's skin. She feels it in her scar, in the
ribs that someone else's knife once opened. A heat that isn't
pain. Recognition.
Yumi sees it. Her breath stops.
The door slides open. Not because someone unlocked it.
Because the building let her go — the way a body expels
something it can no longer contain.
The corridor stretches ahead. Three directions. The facility
hums, alive and aware.

[THIS CHOICE DOES NOT RESOLVE WITHIN SESSION 1]

Behind you: the shattered chamber, Yumi frozen in the

A)

B)
! SESSION 2 OPENS: A room not on any map. Paper screens. Old

C)
! SESSION 2 OPENS: A thumbprint scanner glows blue in a

reflection and your face — waits.
You choose.

your bare feet — the warmth of a city waking up, the hum of
ten million lives beginning another day in a world that is,
as of this morning, slightly less forgetful.
You don't wave. You don't pause.
You fade into the thinning night. Your body vanishes in
silhouette, swallowed by the pulse of the waking world.
But your presence remains.
